Sonic Harm Use of sound, vibration, or acoustic phenomena to harass, injure, surveil, or control. Hearing loss, tinnitus, anxiety, sleep disruption, loss of privacy (voice surveillance). Targeted sonic attacks on ethnic/religious communities; workplace sound harassment. Acoustic weapons in border conflicts; mass voice surveillance eroding civil liberties. Productivity loss from noise-induced illness; litigation costs; audio surveillance market. Normalisation of sonic aggression; erosion of quiet public spaces; chilling effect on free speech.
Chemical Harm Exposure to toxic substances (gases, liquids, solids) that cause injury, illness, or environmental damage. Acute poisoning, chronic disease (e.g. cancer), birth defects. Contamination of drinking water affecting specific neighbourhoods; industrial disasters near vulnerable communities. Chemical warfare; regulatory failures leading to national health crises (e.g. lead in water). Healthcare costs; loss of agricultural yield; cleanup liabilities; trade embargoes. Distrust in public health systems; stigmatisation of affected communities; generational health deficits.
Biological Harm Harm from pathogens, biological agents, or disruption of ecosystems that sustain life. Infectious disease, allergy, bioterrorism injury. Outbreaks in crowded settings (prisons, refugee camps); targeted bioweapon attacks. Pandemics; biological warfare; agricultural blight crippling food security. Pandemic-driven recession; livestock culling; pharmaceutical stockpiling costs; tourism collapse. Social distancing fracturing community bonds; stigmatisation of disease carriers; erosion of trust in science.
Radiological/Nuclear Harm Harm from ionising radiation, radioactive contamination, or nuclear explosions. Radiation sickness, cancer, genetic damage. Displacement of indigenous communities by uranium mining; downwind exposure from testing. Nuclear war; meltdown rendering territory uninhabitable; long-term medical surveillance. Trillion-dollar cleanup (Chernobyl, Fukushima); property abandonment; energy market destabilisation. Intergenerational trauma; creation of permanent exclusion zones; existential risk to civilisation.
Physical/Mechanical Harm Direct injury or death from kinetic forces, machinery, or structural collapse. Trauma, disability, death from assault, accidents, or weapons. Mass shootings; workplace hazards in specific industries; unsafe housing. Conventional warfare; crumbling infrastructure causing national disasters; law enforcement violence. Healthcare and rehabilitation costs; insurance losses; reduced labour force. Desensitisation to violence; militarisation of police; loss of public safety confidence.
Psychological/Emotional Harm Non-physical harm to mental well‑being, including manipulation, coercion, and identity-based attacks. Depression, PTSD, anxiety, suicidal ideation. Bullying in schools; gaslighting in cults; structural discrimination causing minority stress. State-sponsored psychological operations; systemic racism as public health crisis; propaganda eroding mental resilience. Workplace mental health costs; reduced productivity; increased disability claims. Normalisation of hate speech; erosion of empathy; fractured social cohesion.
Economic/Financial Harm Loss or destruction of financial assets, livelihoods, or economic stability through fraud, theft, or systemic shocks. Bankruptcy, eviction, wage theft, pension loss. Predatory lending targeting minority communities; mass layoffs in a region. Sovereign debt crisis; hyperinflation; sanctions crippling national economy. Recession, depression; market crashes; bailout costs; loss of GDP. Rise in inequality; social unrest; loss of intergenerational mobility.
Digital/Information Harm Harm through cyber‑attacks, data breaches, surveillance, disinformation, or algorithmic bias. Identity theft, online harassment, doxing, manipulation. Coordinated harassment campaigns; algorithmic discrimination against protected groups. Election interference; critical infrastructure hacking; mass state surveillance. Ransomware costs; intellectual property theft; erosion of digital commerce trust. Erosion of truth; polarisation; chilling effect on dissent; surveillance capitalism.
Environmental/Ecological Harm Damage to ecosystems, biodiversity, and the life‑supporting functions of the planet. Heat‑related illness, respiratory disease from air pollution, displacement. Loss of ancestral lands for indigenous groups; environmental racism in siting polluting facilities. Climate-related disasters overwhelming national response capacity; loss of territory (rising seas). Agricultural failure; disaster recovery costs; insurance unavailability; stranded assets. Climate migration conflicts; loss of cultural landscapes; existential threat to future generations.
