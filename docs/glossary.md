## **🔹 Harm Categories**

### **Sonic Harm**
- **Definition**: Use of sound, vibration, or acoustic phenomena to harass, injure, surveil, or control individuals or groups.
- **Examples**: LRAD (Long Range Acoustic Device) use at protests, workplace noise harassment, sonic attacks on diplomats.

### **Chemical Harm**
- **Definition**: Exposure to toxic substances (gases, liquids, solids) that cause injury, illness, or environmental damage.
- **Examples**: Flint water crisis, Bhopal gas tragedy, lead contamination in drinking water.

### **Biological Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm from pathogens, biological agents, or disruption of ecosystems that sustain life.
- **Examples**: COVID-19 pandemic, Ebola outbreaks, bioterrorism attacks.

### **Radiological/Nuclear Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm from ionizing radiation, radioactive contamination, or nuclear explosions.
- **Examples**: Chernobyl disaster, Fukushima meltdown, Hiroshima/Nagasaki bombings.

### **Physical/Mechanical Harm**
- **Definition**: Direct injury or death from kinetic forces, machinery, or structural collapse.
- **Examples**: Rana Plaza collapse, mass shootings, workplace accidents.

### **Psychological/Emotional Harm**
- **Definition**: Non-physical harm to mental well-being, including manipulation, coercion, and identity-based attacks.
- **Examples**: Workplace bullying, gaslighting, PTSD from trauma.

### **Economic/Financial Harm**
- **Definition**: Loss or destruction of financial assets, livelihoods, or economic stability through fraud, theft, or systemic shocks.
- **Examples**: 2008 financial crisis, Enron scandal, wage theft.

### **Digital/Information Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm through cyber-attacks, data breaches, surveillance, disinformation, or algorithmic bias.
- **Examples**: WannaCry ransomware, Equifax breach, Cambridge Analytica scandal.

### **Environmental/Ecological Harm**
- **Definition**: Damage to ecosystems, biodiversity, and the life-supporting functions of the planet.
- **Examples**: Amazon deforestation, Deepwater Horizon spill, Australian bushfires.

### **Cultural/Identity Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm targeting cultural, ethnic, or religious identity, including erasure, appropriation, or forced assimilation.
- **Examples**: Canadian residential schools, cultural appropriation in fashion, language extinction.

### **Legal/Systemic Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm arising from the misuse, absence, or weaponization of legal, judicial, or systemic structures.
- **Examples**: SLAPP suits, mass incarceration, discriminatory laws, denial of due process.
- **Key Metric**: **# of SLAPP suits/year** (see [below](#slapp-suit) for details).

### **Institutional Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm resulting from the policies, practices, or negligence of formal organizations (e.g., governments, corporations, religious bodies).
- **Examples**: Carillion collapse, Boeing 737 MAX failures, Catholic Church abuse scandals.

### **Political Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm caused by the abuse of political power, repression, manipulation of democratic processes, or political violence.
- **Examples**: Brexit impact on Ireland, Russian election interference, Myanmar coup.

### **Technological Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm from the development, deployment, or misuse of technology (beyond digital/information contexts).
- **Examples**: Uber’s algorithmic discrimination, AI bias in hiring, autonomous weapons systems.

### **Social Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm that fractures social bonds, community trust, or collective well-being.
- **Examples**: Social media polarization, COVID-19 isolation, gentrification.

### **Reputational Harm**
- **Definition**: Damage to one’s character, honor, or public perception through false information, defamation, or social stigma.
- **Examples**: Twitter (X) brand damage, #MeToo movement, Volkswagen emissions scandal.

### **Organizational Harm**
- **Definition**: Harm arising from toxic workplace cultures, corporate malfeasance, or organizational failures.
- **Examples**: Wells Fargo fake accounts, Amazon warehouse conditions, Enron scandal.

## **📊 Impact Scales**

### **Individual Impact**
- **Definition**: Effects experienced by a single person.
- **Examples**: Hearing loss (Sonic Harm), acute poisoning (Chemical Harm), PTSD (Psychological Harm).

### **Group Impact**
- **Definition**: Effects experienced by a specific community, demographic, or organization.
- **Examples**: Targeted sonic attacks on ethnic communities, mass layoffs, cultural erasure.

### **National Impact**
- **Definition**: Effects that affect an entire country or its systems.
- **Examples**: Nuclear meltdown (Radiological Harm), civil war (Political Harm), sovereign debt crisis (Economic Harm).

### **Economic Impact**
- **Definition**: Financial consequences of harm, including costs, losses, or market disruptions.
- **Examples**: Productivity loss (Sonic Harm), healthcare costs (Chemical Harm), litigation costs (Legal Harm).

### **Societal Impact**
- **Definition**: Broad cultural, social, or behavioral changes resulting from harm.
- **Examples**: Normalization of sonic aggression, erosion of trust in institutions, polarization.

## **⚖️ Severity and Temporal Scales**

### **Severity Levels**
- **Mild**: Low-intensity harm with limited, short-term effects.
  - *Example*: Minor annoyance (Sonic Harm), temporary stress (Psychological Harm).
- **Severe**: Significant harm with lasting or widespread effects.
  - *Example*: Permanent hearing loss (Sonic Harm), wrongful imprisonment (Legal Harm).
- **Catastrophic**: Extreme harm with irreversible or existential consequences.
  - *Example*: Mass disability (Sonic Harm), nuclear winter (Radiological Harm), genocide (Political Harm).

### **Temporal Scales**
- **Immediate**: Effects that occur within days or weeks.
  - *Example*: Acute poisoning (Chemical Harm), data breach (Digital Harm).
- **Chronic**: Ongoing or long-term effects.
  - *Example*: Chronic illness (Chemical Harm), workplace bullying (Psychological Harm).
- **Intergenerational**: Effects that span multiple generations.
  - *Example*: Genetic damage (Radiological Harm), cultural erasure (Cultural/Identity Harm).
- **Existential**: Threats to the survival or fundamental well-being of humanity or ecosystems.
  - *Example*: Climate change (Environmental Harm), AI misalignment (Technological Harm).

## **🛠️ Framework-Specific Terms**

### **Enablers/Root Causes**
- **Definition**: Systemic factors that allow harm to occur or persist.
- **Examples**: Corporate lobbying (Chemical Harm), weak labor laws (Organizational Harm), lack of accountability (Institutional Harm).

### **Mitigation/Response**
- **Definition**: Actions or interventions to prevent, reduce, or address harm.
- **Examples**: Noise ordinances (Sonic Harm), anti-SLAPP legislation (Legal Harm), whistleblower protections (Institutional Harm).

### **Case Studies**
- **Definition**: Real-world examples illustrating each harm category.
- **Purpose**: Provide context and evidence for understanding harm impacts.
- **Examples**: Flint water crisis (Chemical Harm), Cambridge Analytica (Digital Harm).

### **Metrics/KPIs**
- **Definition**: Quantifiable measurements to track the occurrence, severity, or impact of harm.
- **Purpose**: Enable data-driven analysis and monitoring.
- **Examples**: # of SLAPP suits/year (Legal Harm), CO₂ emissions (Environmental Harm), injury rates (Physical Harm).

### **Data Sources**
- **Definition**: Authoritative providers of data for metrics.
- **Purpose**: Ensure reliability and reproducibility of harm assessments.
- **Examples**: WHO (Biological Harm), EPA (Chemical Harm), Freedom House (Political Harm).

## **🔬 Specialized Terms**

### **SLAPP Suit**
- **Definition**: **Strategic Lawsuit Against Public Participation** – a lawsuit or legal threat intended to intimidate and silence critics by burdening them with the cost of a legal defense.
- **Context**: A key metric in **Legal/Systemic Harm** (# of SLAPP suits/year).
- **Data Sources**: Index on Censorship, Reporters Without Borders, national court records.

### **R₀ (R-naught)**
- **Definition**: **Basic Reproduction Number** – the average number of people one infected person will pass a virus to in a population where no one is immune.
- **Context**: Metric for **Biological Harm** (e.g., COVID-19 R₀ ~2.5).

### **Algorithmic Discrimination**
- **Definition**: Bias or unfair outcomes resulting from the use of algorithms, often in hiring, lending, or law enforcement.
- **Context**: A form of **Digital/Information Harm** or **Technological Harm**.

### **Surveillance Capitalism**
- **Definition**: Economic system centered around the commodification of personal data, typically used for targeted advertising or manipulation.
- **Context**: A driver of **Digital/Information Harm** and **Reputational Harm**.

### **Digital Colonialism**
- **Definition**: The domination of digital infrastructure and data by wealthy nations or corporations, often at the expense of less powerful regions.
- **Context**: An **enabler** of **Technological Harm** and **Economic Harm**.

### **Chilling Effect**
- **Definition**: The suppression of free speech or civic participation due to fear of legal, social, or physical repercussions.
- **Context**: A **societal impact** of **Legal/Systemic Harm** and **Reputational Harm**.

### **Existential Risk**
- **Definition**: A risk that threatens the destruction of humanity’s long-term potential or the permanent collapse of civilization.
- **Context**: **Radiological/Nuclear Harm**, **Environmental Harm**, **Technological Harm**.

### **Environmental Racism**
- **Definition**: The disproportionate impact of environmental hazards on communities of color or marginalized groups.
- **Context**: An **enabler** of **Environmental/Ecological Harm** and **Chemical Harm**.

### **Regulatory Capture**
- **Definition**: When a regulatory agency, created to act in the public interest, instead advances the commercial or political concerns of special interest groups.
- **Context**: An **enabler** of **Institutional Harm** and **Economic Harm**. 

### **Brain Drain**
- **Definition**: The emigration of educated or professional individuals from a country or region, often due to political instability or lack of opportunity.
- **Context**: A **national impact** of **Political Harm** and **Economic Harm**.

## **📈 Data and Metrics Terms**

### **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
- **Definition**: A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively a system or organization is achieving key objectives.
- **Context**: Used to track **mitigation efforts** (e.g., % reduction in SLAPP suits after legislation).

### **ppm (Parts Per Million)**
- **Definition**: A unit of concentration, often used to measure pollutants in air, water, or soil.
- **Context**: Metric for **Chemical Harm** and **Environmental Harm**. 

### **Decibel (dB)**
- **Definition**: A logarithmic unit used to measure the intensity of sound.
- **Context**: Metric for **Sonic Harm** (>85dB is considered harmful).

### **Sievert (Sv)**
- **Definition**: A unit of ionizing radiation dose in the International System of Units (SI).
- **Context**: Metric for **Radiological/Nuclear Harm**. 

### **GDP Loss (%)**
- **Definition**: The percentage decrease in a country’s Gross Domestic Product due to harm (e.g., pandemic, financial crisis).
- **Context**: Metric for **Economic/Financial Harm**.
