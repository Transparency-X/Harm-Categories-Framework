## 🔟 Proposed Sensory Harm Registries

### 1. **Haptic / Tactile Harm** (Touch)
**The sense:** Pressure, texture, vibration, and skin‑to‑skin contact.  
**Harm vectors:**  
- Vibration white finger (hand‑arm vibration syndrome)  
- Contact burns, frostbite, chemical burns  
- Tactile deprivation (e.g., solitary confinement, sensory isolation)  
- Texture‑based torture (sandpaper, rough surfaces)  
- Phantom limb pain  
- Forced tickling  
- Haptic feedback weaponised in VR suits  

### 2. **Olfactory Harm** (Smell)
**The sense:** Detection of volatile chemicals in the air.  
**Harm vectors:**  
- Malodour attacks (skunk, cadaverine) used as harassment  
- Olfactory-triggered migraines or asthma  
- Deliberate use of scent to manipulate emotions (predatory marketing, gaslighting)  
- Olfactory fatigue / anosmia from workplace chemical exposure  
- Psychological trauma linked to specific smells (PTSD triggers)  
- Olfactory hallucinations (phantosmia) causing distress  

### 3. **Gustatory Harm** (Taste)
**The sense:** Sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami via the tongue.  
**Harm vectors:**  
- Poisoning via taste‑masked substances  
- Bitterness‑based punishment (Denatonium benzoate coatings)  
- Dysgeusia (persistent bad taste) from medication or disease  
- Force‑feeding with unpleasant flavours (torture)  
- Taste aversion conditioning to cause malnutrition  
- Gustatory hallucinations (e.g., metallic taste in psychosis)  

### 4. **Vestibular / Balance Harm**
**The sense:** Head position, motion, and equilibrium (inner ear).  
**Harm vectors:**  
- VR‑induced cybersickness (already in Visual Harm, but with pure vestibular dimension)  
- Mal de debarquement syndrome from prolonged motion  
- Vestibular torture (spinning, “the German chair”)  
- Unsafe rollercoaster/amusement park incidents  
- Vertigo attacks induced by sound or visual flicker (cross‑sense)  
- Chronic dizziness from ototoxic drugs  

### 5. **Thermoceptive Harm** (Temperature)
**The sense:** Heat and cold perceived by the skin and internal organs.  
**Harm vectors:**  
- Burns (heat, sun, radiation) – already in Physical Harm, but could be a sensory sub‑type  
- Frostbite and hypothermia  
- Thermal shock used as punishment (alternating hot and cold water)  
- Sauna/steam room overheating without consent  
- Thermal hallucinations (intense cold as pain)  
- Indoor workplace heat stress (chronic)  

### 6. **Nociceptive / Pain Harm** (A dedicated pain sense)
**The sense:** Pain is now recognised as a distinct sensory modality (nociception).  
**Harm vectors:**  
- All forms of torture that target pain directly  
- Chronic pain syndromes from nerve damage  
- Pain‑inducing VR/AR devices (haptics that cause real pain)  
- Indifference to pain through drugs, used to enable self‑harm  
- Pain as a social signal (public punishment, “pain compliance” policing)  

### 7. **Proprioceptive / Kinesthetic Harm**
**The sense:** Body position, movement, and limb location.  
**Harm vectors:**  
- Whiplash and joint hyperextension  
- Confusion of body schema via mirrors, illusions, or VR (rubber hand illusion, body transfer)  
- Proprioceptive degradation in microgravity or bed rest  
- Deliberate misalignment of limbs during restraint  
- Phantom limb pain (already in haptic) – but also a body‑map distortion  
- Vestibular‑proprioceptive conflict causing nausea and disorientation  

### 8. **Interoceptive Harm** (Internal organs)
**The sense:** Sensations from inside the body (hunger, thirst, heartbeat, breathing).  
**Harm vectors:**  
- Starvation (hunger as weapon)  
- Dehydration (thirst torture)  
- Dyspnoea (air hunger) induced chemically or mechanically  
- Palpitations and visceral distress from fear conditioning  
- Interoceptive hallucinations (feeling insects under skin, false pregnancy)  

### 9. **Chronoceptive Harm** (Time perception) – more abstract, but still sensory
**The sense:** Subjective passage of time.  
**Harm vectors:**  
- Solitary confinement destroying time perception  
- Dopamine‑related disorders (Parkinson’s, ADHD) affecting time estimation  
- Virtual reality time compression causing neglect of real‑world needs  
- Sensory deprivation tanks leading to distorted time experience
